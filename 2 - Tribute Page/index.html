Bruce Lee

Bruce Lee (1940-1973) was a Hong Kong-American martial artist, actor, director, and philosopher, widely regarded as one of the most influential martial artists of all time. He founded the martial art philosophy of Jeet Kune Do, emphasizing adaptability and practicality. Through films like Enter the Dragon and The Way of the Dragon, he broke cultural barriers and introduced martial arts cinema to a global audience. His legacy continues to inspire generations around the world, not only in martial arts but also in fitness, film, and philosophy.
